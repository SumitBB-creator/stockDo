MODULE: PURCHASES - EXHAUSTIVE DETAILS

1. OVERVIEW
The Purchases module manages Capital Expenditure (CAPEX). It records the acquisition of new materials from external suppliers. Financially, it acts as the "Payables" record, ensuring that any money owed to a supplier for new inventory is tracked via the general ledger and dashboard summaries.

2. DATABASE TABLES
--- Table: purchases ---
* Purpose: Stores the header information for a procurement bill from a supplier.
* Critical Fields:
  - id (UUID): Internal primary key.
  - purchaseNumber (String, e.g., "PUR-0005"): Automated sequential ID for internal tracking.
  - billNumber (String): The physical invoice number provided by the supplier.
  - date (DateTime): The date of the acquisition.
  - supplierId (UUID): Links to the master Supplier record.
  - totalAmount / grandTotal: Financial totals (pre-tax and post-tax).
  - GST Fields: cgst, sgst, igst, gstType, gstRate.
  - status (String): "Draft" or "Finalized".
* Relationships:
  - items: Links to the specific materials and quantities bought.

--- Table: purchase_items ---
* Purpose: Records the individual lines of a purchase.
* Fields:
  - description: Details of the material.
  - quantity (Int): How many units were bought.
  - rate: The purchase price per unit.
  - materialId (UUID, Optional): Links the line item to the master Material list. This link is primarily for historical price tracking.

3. FILE-BY-FILE CODE EXPLANATION

--- backend/src/purchases/purchases.service.ts ---
* Logic: Supplier Ledger Integration (`create` method)
  - When a purchase is saved, the service automatically initiates a **CREDIT** transaction in the Supplier's ledger.
  - This increases the amount the company "Owes" to that supplier.
  - This transaction is linked via `referenceId`, allowing the user to click a ledger entry and view the original purchase details.
* Logic: Atomic Transaction
  - Uses `this.prisma.$transaction` to ensure that a purchase record and its corresponding ledger transaction are either both saved or both fail.
* Logic: Purchase Sequencing
  - It uses a custom split/parse logic on `PUR-XXXX` strings to find the next available numeric ID.
* Logic: Missing Auto-Stock Update (IMPORTANT)
  - Analysis of the service shows that creating a Purchase **does NOT** automatically update `material.totalQty`.
  - The Purchases module is currently focused on the accounting side (Accounts Payable).
  - Users must manually update the Material's `totalQty` in the Materials Master module after a physical delivery is received.

--- backend/src/purchases/dto/create-purchase.dto.ts ---
* Logic: Financial Validation
  - Ensures a `supplierId` and `totalAmount` are provided.
  - Validates nested items to ensure that quantity, rate, and amount calculations are mathematically consistent before reaching the database.

4. CORE BUSINESS LOGIC & FLOW
1. Logistial Trigger: New materials arrive at the warehouse.
2. Data Entry: Staff record the supplier's bill in the "New Purchase" screen.
3. Accounting: The system creates the Purchase record and updates the Supplier’s balance in the balance sheet.
4. Profit & Loss: The total purchase amount is used by the `TransactionsService` to calculate the company's "Spendings" in dashboard visualizations and annual reports.
