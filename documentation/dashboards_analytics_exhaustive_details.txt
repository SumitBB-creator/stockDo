MODULE: DASHBOARDS & BUSINESS ANALYTICS - EXHAUSTIVE DETAILS

1. OVERVIEW
The Dashboards & Analytics module is the intelligence layer of StockDo. It aggregates data from every other module (Billing, Inventory, Agreements, Transactions) to provide a real-time health check of the business. It is responsible for the high-level charts, profit margins, and automated stock alerts.

2. ANALYTIC ENGINES

--- The Revenue Snapshot Engine (Cron Job) ---
* Logic: `DailyRevenueService`
  - Purpose: Tracks "Potential Revenue" even when no bills are generated.
  - Cycle: Runs every day at midnight (@Cron).
  - Algorithm: 
    1. Fetches all "Active" agreements.
    2. Reconstructs the current on-field stock for each customer by replaying their Challan history (Issues minus Returns).
    3. Multiplies the live stock by the specific `hireRate` agreed upon in the contract.
    4. Saves the daily total to the `daily_revenue` table.
* Value: Powers the "Daily Revenue Trend" area chart on the dashboard.

--- The Financial Aggregator ---
* Logic: `TransactionsService`
  - Dashboard Summary: Calculates annual "Earnings" (Finalized Bills) vs "Spendings" (Purchases + Expenses) to show the Net Profit and Margin %.
  - Gst Summary: Real-time calculation of tax liabilities by comparing GST collected from customers vs GST paid to suppliers.
  - Balance Sheet: A complex method that dynamically evaluates Assets (Fixed Assets + Stock Valuation + Debtors) vs Liabilities (Creditors + Customer Advances).

3. FRONTEND VISUALIZATION
* Technology: `Recharts` library.
* Components:
  - Monthly Performance: A Bar Chart showing a month-by-month comparison of earnings vs spendings.
  - Daily Revenue Trend: An Area Chart using the snapshots generated by the Cron job.
  - Shortage Alerts: A logic loop that compares "Available Stock" (Total - Issued) against the "Lower Limit" threshold for every material.

4. CORE BUSINESS LOGIC & FLOW
1. Automation: Every midnight, the system "counts" the money sitting on customer sites.
2. Alerting: As soon as a material's available quantity drops below its safety limit, the Dashboard raises a "Red/Amber" alert.
3. Decision Making: The owner uses the "Annual Report" to see which months generate the highest profit and identifies high-loss periods due to material damage or shortages.
4. Tax Compliance: The GST summary allows the accountant to prepare monthly filings by aggregating hundreds of line items into single Taxable/CGST/SGST/IGST totals.
