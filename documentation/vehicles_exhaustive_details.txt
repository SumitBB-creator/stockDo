MODULE: VEHICLES - EXHAUSTIVE DETAILS

1. OVERVIEW
The Vehicles module provides fleet management and compliance tracking. Its primary purpose beyond simple asset tracking is to manage the "Compliance Lifecycle" of company-owned vehicles by alerting staff about critical upcoming regulatory deadlines (Insurance, Permits, Taxes, etc.).

2. DATABASE TABLES
--- Table: vehicles ---
* Purpose: Stores master records for all company vehicles and their regulatory due dates.
* Critical Fields:
  - vehicleNumber (String, Unique): The registration number.
  - vehicleType (String): Car, Truck, Crane, etc.
  - Status (String): Active/Inactive.
* Compliance Fields (Due Dates):
  - nextServiceDue: Date for mechanical maintenance.
  - pollutionDue: Expiry for PUC (Pollution Under Control).
  - insuranceDue: Expiry for vehicle insurance coverage.
  - roadTaxDue: Expiry for state road tax payments.
  - tokenTaxDue: Expiry for commercial token taxes.
  - nationalPermitDue / statePermitDue: Regulatory permissions for transport.
  - fitnessTestDue: Mandatory commercial vehicle fitness certification.

3. FILE-BY-FILE CODE EXPLANATION

--- backend/src/vehicles/vehicles.service.ts ---
* Logic: Lightweight CRUD
  - The service provides standard Create, Read, Update, Delete (CRUD) operations.
  - It handles ISO date strings for all compliance fields, storing them as standard DateTime objects in Postgres.
  - It sorts the vehicle list by most recently added (`createdAt: 'desc'`).

--- backend/src/vehicles/dto/create-vehicle.dto.ts ---
* Logic: Schema Enforcement
  - Uses `@IsDateString()` to ensure that frontend inputs for compliance dates are valid year-month-day formats.
  - This prevents data corruption in the "Due Date" engine.

--- frontend/src/app/dashboard/vehicles/page.tsx ---
* Logic: Compliance Dashboard (Frontend Logic)
  - This is where the "Intelligence" of the module resides.
  - Alert logic: The UI compares the stored `Due Dates` against the current system date.
  - Visual Indicators: Vehicles with dates in the past or approaching soon (e.g., within 15-30 days) are highlighted as "Critical" or "Warning" to prompt the fleet manager to take action.

4. CORE BUSINESS LOGIC & FLOW
1. Asset Creation: When a new vehicle is bought, it is recorded with all its initial permit and tax expiry dates.
2. Routine Updates: After a vehicle is serviced or a tax is paid, the user updates the specific "Due Date" field to the next expiry.
3. Operations: On every login, the dashboard (via the vehicles page) scans these dates to ensure the company never operates a vehicle with expired insurance or permits, which would be a major legal risk.
